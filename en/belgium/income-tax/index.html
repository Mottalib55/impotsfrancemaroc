Complete Guide to Belgian Taxation 2026

Belgium has one of the most complex and highest tax systems in Europe. The IPP (Impot des Personnes Physiques / Personal Income Tax) is a progressive federal tax, supplemented by municipal surcharges (centimes additionnels) ranging from 6% to 9% depending on your municipality of residence.

IPP Tax Brackets 2026

Belgian income tax uses a marginal bracket system. Only the portion of income within each bracket is taxed at that rate:

  • EUR 0 - 15,820: 25%
  • EUR 15,820 - 27,920: 40%
  • EUR 27,920 - 48,320: 45%
  • Above EUR 48,320: 50%

Municipal surcharges (6-9% of IPP) are added on top, which can push the effective marginal rate above 53%.

Marital Quotient and Child Allowances

Marital Quotient (Quotient Conjugal)

If your spouse has little or no income, you can allocate up to 30% of your professional income (max ~EUR 11,000) to reduce your overall tax burden. This is calculated automatically by the tax administration.

Pension Savings: Tax Advantage

Pension Savings Tax Reduction

  • Option 1: Max contribution EUR 1,020/year = 30% tax reduction (EUR 306)
  • Option 2: Max contribution EUR 1,310/year = 25% tax reduction (EUR 327.50)
  • Pension savings allow you to build retirement capital while receiving immediate tax benefits
  • Funds are locked until age 60 (advantageous taxation at withdrawal)

Other Tax Deductions

  • Professional expenses (flat rate): 30% of income (max EUR 5,520) or actual costs with receipts
  • Service vouchers (titres-services): 30% tax reduction for approved domestic help
  • Donations: 45% tax reduction (minimum EUR 40 per institution)
  • Regional housing bonus: Mortgage interest and capital deduction (varies by region)
  • ALE vouchers: Tax reduction for local community work
