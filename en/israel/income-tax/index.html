Complete Guide to Israeli Taxation

Israel has a progressive income tax system with 7 tax brackets, ranging from 10% to 50%. The system is administered by the Israel Tax Authority (Rashut HaMisim). Israeli taxation is based on the residency principle: Israeli residents are taxed on their worldwide income, while non-residents are taxed only on Israeli-source income.

Income Tax Brackets 2026 (Mas Hachnasa)

Israeli income tax uses a progressive system with seven brackets:

  • ILS 0 - ILS 84,120: 10%
  • ILS 84,121 - ILS 120,720: 14%
  • ILS 120,721 - ILS 193,800: 20%
  • ILS 193,801 - ILS 269,280: 31%
  • ILS 269,281 - ILS 560,280: 35%
  • ILS 560,281 - ILS 721,560: 47%
  • Above ILS 721,560: 50%

Note: The brackets are updated annually based on the Consumer Price Index.

Health Tax (Mas Briut)

Mandatory Health Insurance

  • Up to 60% of average wage: ~3.1%
  • Above 60% of average wage: ~5%
  • Provides universal healthcare coverage through health funds (Kupat Holim)
  • All Israeli residents are covered by the National Health Insurance Law

Tax Credits (Nekudot Zikui)

Tax Credit Points System

  • Each credit point worth approximately ILS 2,904/year (2026)
  • Israeli resident: 2.25 points
  • Woman: additional 0.5 points
  • Children: 1.5-2.5 points per child depending on age
  • Single parent: additional 1 point
  • New immigrant (Oleh Hadash): 3 points for 18 months, then 2 points for 12 months, then 1 point for 12 months
  • Academic degree: 1 point for 3 years after graduation

New Immigrant Benefits (Oleh Hadash)

Special Tax Benefits for Olim

  • 10-year exemption on foreign-source income and capital gains
  • Tax credit points as mentioned above
  • Reduced tax rate on foreign pensions
  • No reporting requirement for foreign assets during benefit period
  • Benefits apply to both Olim (Jewish immigrants) and returning residents

Pension and Savings Deductions

Tax-Advantaged Savings

  • Pension fund (Keren Pensia): mandatory employer contributions, tax-deductible
  • Provident fund (Kupat Gemel): additional savings option
  • Education fund (Keren Hishtalmut): tax-free after 6 years, very popular
  • Life insurance: certain premiums are tax-deductible
  • Employer must contribute at least 6.5% to pension

Tax Filing and Calendar

  • Tax year: Calendar year (January 1 - December 31)
  • Filing deadline: April 30 for most taxpayers, May 31 online
  • Employers submit: Annual 106 form to employees
  • Self-employed: Must file annual return and make advance payments
  • Online system: Shaam (personal area on Tax Authority website)
  • Refund claims: Up to 6 years retroactively

High-Tech Industry Tax Benefits

Tech Sector Incentives

  • Stock options (102 track): Favorable tax treatment for employee options
  • R&D grants: Israel Innovation Authority funding
  • Preferred enterprise: Reduced corporate tax rate of 7.5% for qualifying companies
  • IP regime: Favorable treatment for intellectual property
  • Israel is known as the "Startup Nation" with significant tax incentives for tech
